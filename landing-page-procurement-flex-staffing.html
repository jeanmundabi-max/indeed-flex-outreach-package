The Problem — Fill Rate Failure
"Our incumbent supplier can't fill shifts during peak. We had 40 no-shows at Christmas and it cost us the whole festive period."

That is not a staffing problem. That is a supplier dependency problem. When your contingent workforce sits on a preferred supplier who cannot scale past a single platform, every major event is a gamble. You find out on the morning of the shift — not three weeks before when you could have done something about it.

For a 500-person hospitality operation running 50 temp shifts per week, a single event fill-rate failure means lost revenue, emergency agency calls at premium rates, and a conversation with the board you did not want to have.

40 no-shows × £200 revenue per shift = £8,000 lost in a single peak event
The Problem — Compliance Exposure
"We have compliance exposure with the agency workers we use. Someone from HMRC came asking questions about IR35 misclassification. That is the one that keeps me up at night."

The IR35 misclassification risk is real. Agency workers placed without compliant contracts, without verifiable employment status checks, and without audit trails create liability that sits on your balance sheet — not your agency's. One enforcement case costs between £10,000 and £100,000 before you account for reputational damage.

The problem is compounded when your current setup involves multiple agencies, multiple portals, and no single view of who is working where and under what contract. Switching to a new platform only solves this if the compliance tracking is built in — not bolted on. Right to work, DBS, IR35 status, and contract documentation all need to exist before the worker walks in the door.

IR35 misclassification case: £10,000 to £100,000+ per enforcement action

Indeed Flex works best for operations-heavy employers with regular or seasonal contingent workforce needs. If you run a single-site business with fewer than 20 temp shifts per month, the platform is available but the economics work best at scale.

You have IR35 exposure in your current agency arrangement. You cannot tell your finance director exactly what your temp labour compliance status is. You need vetting, right-to-work, and contract compliance tracked — not managed by a rota manager with a spreadsheet.

Strong fit

The Multi-Site Cost Problem

You operate across 3 or more sites and temp labour is a significant cost line. Procurement has asked you to consolidate suppliers and reduce the total contingent workforce bill. You need a Vendor Management System, not another agency portal.

Not the right fit

Common Questions

The questions operations and procurement teams ask before switching

We have a preferred supplier agreement. Procurement will take 90 days to onboard a new supplier. How do we manage this? +
Indeed Flex is designed to work alongside your existing PSL, not replace it overnight. You can start with a pilot on specific sites or shift types while procurement runs the formal onboarding process in parallel. Most enterprise clients are filling shifts within the first week while the formal PSL process completes in the background. Flex Plus also integrates your existing agency relationships into the VMS, so you do not have to terminate current suppliers to use the platform.
Our workers are familiar with our sites. Starting again with a new pool is a risk. +
Your existing casual workers can be invited directly to your Indeed Flex worker bank. They join the platform, complete compliance verification, and continue working your shifts — now through a system that tracks their records and pays them same-day. The transition is additive, not a replacement. Workers who worked your sites before become your rated, preferred pool on the platform.
We need a TUPE assessment if we move the whole workforce. Does this apply? +
TUPE applies to the transfer of an undertaking, not the adoption of a staffing platform. Moving to Indeed Flex from an agency arrangement does not trigger TUPE in the same way as a service transfer. Your employment legal team can confirm this for your specific arrangement. Indeed Flex's compliance team has handled this question for enterprise clients and can provide supporting documentation for your legal review.
My board wants me to cut costs, not add another platform subscription. How does this work financially? +
The platform cost is offset by the agency premium reduction. At a 25% saving on a typical annual temp labour bill of £1.56 million, the annual saving is £390,000. The Flex Plus pricing for enterprise accounts is structured as a percentage of managed spend — so the platform only costs more when you are saving more. The first-year saving consistently exceeds the platform cost for enterprise clients. We can model this for your specific labour bill before you sign anything.
Can you prove you can fill on the same day in our location? +
Indeed Flex operates across the UK with 240,000+ pre-vetted workers. Before onboarding, we run a worker density check for your specific locations and role types. If the density does not meet your requirements, we tell you before you commit — not after. For hospitality and catering across major UK cities and their surrounding areas, the fill rate holds above 95% on same-day requests.
